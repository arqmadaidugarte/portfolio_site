About

Architecture that serves people.

I believe architecture's highest purpose is to serve people — not as an abstract ideal, but as the active starting point of every design decision. I'm an architect and architectural visualization designer graduating from Universidad de Los Andes (2025), where my thesis explored neuroarchitecture as a framework for social relationship-based design. My work combines technical precision with cinematic storytelling, translating complex spatial ideas into images that communicate meaning before a single wall is built.

Cultural Museum • Academic Project Year 4 • 2024

Ecomuseo
de los Andes

LocationParque Metropolitano Albarregas, Mérida
RoleSole Architect
SoftwareTwinmotion + Archicad + Gemini Agents
ReferenceMuseo de Arte Contemporáneo de Suzhou — Bjarke Ingels
Concept Process Technical Outcome
01

Concept

A portal of access to the Parque Metropolitano Albarregas. The fragmented form responds to topography, solar incidence, and the vernacular language of informal settlements — a building that belongs to its landscape while reinterpreting it.

02

Process

The design evolution moves through six formal operations — rotation, division, stepping, extrusion, and deformation — each responding to a specific environmental or programmatic driver.

03

Technical

Architectural drawings spanning five floor levels (±0.00 to -12.00), four facade elevations, and one longitudinal section.

Civic Campus • Undergraduate Thesis • 2025

LBN Las Buenas
Nuevas Campus

LocationMérida, Venezuela
RoleSole Architect
SoftwareTwinmotion + Archicad + Gemini Agents
FrameworkNeuroarchitecture + Faith Community
Concept Process Technical Outcome
01

Concept

A faith campus designed from neuroarchitecture principles. Three relational pillars — Capacitación (low intimacy), Cuidado (medium intimacy), and Celebración (theater) — organize around an octagonal relational center. References: River Arena Church, Glasir Tórshavn, Teatro Teresa Carreño, Steven Holl (porosity).

02

Process

Hand-drawn sketches from the design notebook document the spatial and structural thinking at each stage — from initial zoning to detailed construction sections. Scroll to explore.

03

Technical

Annotated architectural models communicate materiality, spatial program, structural systems, and chromatic strategy across all three building pillars.

04

Outcome

Photorealistic renders capturing the campus across time — golden hour arrival, nocturnal intimacy, and the lived energy of each programmatic space.

RoleConcept + Form + Visualization
SoftwareTwinmotion + Gemini Agents

Concept Process Technical Outcome
01

Concept

Two cylinders that embrace — a metaphor of social cohesion between a vulnerable neighborhood and the middle class. The park penetrates the building. Seven levels of program designed for cyclists, runners, and families.

02

Process

Architectural models reveal the spatial logic of each level — read top to bottom, from the aerial view of the complete campus down through each programmatic floor.

03

Technical Drawings

Complete architectural drawing set — seven floor plans from level -5.00 to +19.00, plus two longitudinal sections. Hover to expand, click to view full size.
